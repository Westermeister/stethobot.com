Heart Disease Diagnosis

Prerequisites

In order to diagnose heart disease, Stethobot requires the following 13 values about the patient:

  1. Age in years.
  2. Biological sex i.e. male or female.
  3. Chest pain type i.e. one of the following:
    • typical angina
    • atypical angina
    • non-anginal pain
    • asymptomatic
  4. Resting blood pressure in mmHg on admission to hospital.
  5. Serum cholesterol in mg/dl.
  6. Whether or not fasting blood sugar > 120 mg/dl.
  7. Resting electrocardiographic results i.e. one of the following:
    • normal
    • ST-T wave abnormality (T wave inversions and/or ST elevation or depression of > 0.05mV)
    • probable or definite left ventricular hypertrophy by Estes' criteria
  8. Maximum heart rate achieved.
  9. Exercised induced angina: yes or no?
  10. ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest. ST relates to positions on the ECG plot. Learn more here.
  11. Slope of the peak exercise ST segment i.e. one of the following:
    • upsloping
    • flat
    • downsloping
  12. Number of major blood vessels (0-3) colored by flourosopy.
  13. Thalassemia i.e. one of the following:
    • 3 = normal
    • 6 = fixed defect
    • 7 = reversable defect

For more information, please check out the source of the dataset used to train this diagnosis model.

All fields are required.
