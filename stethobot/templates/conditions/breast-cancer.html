Breast Cancer Diagnosis

Prerequisites

In order to diagnose breast cancer, Stethobot needs a series of measurements from a grayscale image of a fine needle aspirate of a breast mass. These measurements describe characteristics of the cell nuclei present in the image. In particular, Stethobot requires the means, standard errors, and maximums of the following 10 values:

  1. Radius (mean of distances from center to points on perimeter)
  2. Texture (standard deviation of grayscale values)
  3. Perimeter
  4. Area
  5. Smoothness (local variation in raidus lengths)
  6. Compactness (perimeter^2 / (area - 1))
  7. Concavity (severity of concave portions of the contour)
  8. Concave points (number of concave portions of the contour)
  9. Symmetry
  10. Fractal dimension ("coastline approximation" - 1)

If any of this is unclear in any way, Stethobot hosts a copy of the official documentation from the creators of the dataset that the diagnosis model is fit on. This documentation, from the University of Wisconsin-Madison, goes more in-depth into how to procure these values. Please read it thoroughly.

Please prepare these values, preferably with 4 or more significant digits. Then, enter the mean, standard error, and maximum as a comma-separated list e.g. 1,2,3 for each field shown below. Examples are provided for each field. All fields are required.
